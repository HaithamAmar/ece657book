\section*{Acknowledgments}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Acknowledgments}

\noindent
This book grew out of teaching ECE~657 (Modern Intelligent Systems) at the University
of Waterloo and of revising the notes across multiple offerings until they could
stand on their own as a coherent reference. I am especially grateful to
Prof.~Fakhri Karray, who built ECE~657 and generously invited me to broaden its
scope---an act of intellectual generosity that made this book possible.

\medskip
\noindent
I thank the students who kept me honest. Their questions (often asked at exactly
the wrong time in a derivation) exposed where notation was overloaded, where an
argument quietly skipped a step, and where an example needed numbers rather than
slogans. Many of the ``audit'' habits emphasized throughout the book---sanity
checks, edge-case testing, and careful reporting---reflect the kinds of failures
students encountered when implementations did not match the math.

\medskip
\noindent
I also thank colleagues in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering
who shared perspective on how these topics fit together as an engineering
discipline rather than as isolated techniques, and who encouraged a book-length
treatment. The open-source ecosystem around \LaTeX{}, Python, and scientific
computing made it possible to cross-check derivations and reproduce figures with
minimal friction. If you have used this text in a course offering, the teaching
assistants and staff who supported the logistics deserve credit as well; any
offering-specific details are intentionally separated into a course-edition build.

\medskip
\noindent
Finally, I am grateful to readers who provided detailed feedback on clarity,
typography, and scientific consistency. Any remaining errors and omissions are
my responsibility.
